Drawing History

A Philosophical Exploration

Introduction

History is not a straight line. It's a complex, intertwining series of events that shape our world. This website explores the ideas of history's structure, direction, and the unpredictable paths it takes.

Fractures in the World Order

Global alliances are fracturing, and hard-right movements are rising. These events make us question the inevitability of progress.

Circular and Cyclical Views

Many cultures and philosophers, like Nietzsche and the Stoics, envision history as a cycle. This section explores the idea of history repeating itself.

Dialectics and Opposites

Hegel and Marx described history as a series of conflicts resolved at higher levels. This section delves into the dialectical nature of historical progress.

The Spirograph Theory

Imagine history as a loop spiral, constantly crossing itself and becoming more complex. This section presents the Spirograph theory of history.
